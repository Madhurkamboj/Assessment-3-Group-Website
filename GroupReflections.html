Group Reflection

Our group started with Emma deciding to form one in week 3 with Cody, Jing and Daniel . Madhur joined soon after, as well as Anna in week 5, making 6 members. Cody did a good job chairing the first meeting, but had to withdraw from the course after an urgent family situation came up. Emma also dropped out after the second meeting. Anna and Madhur managed to find two more team members, Muhammed and Mark, and we decided on the team name as well starting work on the report. Things went well, with Jing and Anna doing administrative tasks and organization while Madhur focused most of his efforts on the website. Daniel unfortunately had to withdraw from the course after having to leave for his job on short notice. Our team has had its fair share of ups and downs, and has seen a few people leave the group, but despite that we have still managed to recover quickly and stay on schedule. Effective teamwork is the key to any successful business, and a good skill to learn if you're going to break into any industry. Everyone needs to know how to work in a team, and be able to communicate well, collaborate, set up goals and make decisions together, etc. This assignment has given us good practice in developing those skills, though there are still some areas we could work on, like communicating clearly to make sure that everyone understands what they need to do, being able to see reasonable goals and stick to them, and finalizing decisions earlier. Getting ideas across can be difficult when out primary contact with each other is online. Face-to-face communication allow us to seeing other's body language, and helps us to see the other members as real people. During this assignment we have gotten good practice in using Microsoft Teams and organizing our work there. We believe that seeing everyone’s strength is very important in the teamwork. In our group, Madhur can build a wonderful website and Anna has good design ideas; those wonderful skills can help the other members focus on the other tasks. Using each member’s strengths will improve the project's quality and speed up solutions to a problem. Our team might still have room to improve, and it would be nice to see more member of our group take initiative in the future. Trusting your team members is a very important in teamwork. It makes the team work together better and makes the experience less stressful for everyone involved. In such short practice period, our group’s members were able to establish a good relationship, but there was still some stress and anxiety over miscommunication. Managing a group where everyone has an equal say is hard. And our group did its best, though our efforts are still not perfect. Most of our team has had their turn to charge the meeting and set up small tasks for the next meeting. The team made efforts to help members who were lost or falling behind. This practice is a good sign to see everyone’s strength and make it useful if we respect individuals.
