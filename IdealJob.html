What common elements are there, if any?



Of all of the ideal jobs Team Alpha has selected, there are some notable features shared among them. These include:
--All of them mainly focus on and require proficiency with technology, as to be expected in an IT job.
--All of them involve being able to work in or manage a team. Teamwork and being able to collaborate is an important skill in any kind of job, and as this course emphasizes group work, it will be good opportunity to practice and develop this skill.
--Most of them are in the field of software and/or require experience in some sort of programming language. This is a commonly sought after skill in the IT industry and an expected requirement of a job in this field.
--Some of them emphasize creativity and problem solving as desirable skills for their respective positions, while others emphasize good interpersonal skills.

What differentiates each position from the others, if anything?



While all the jobs are in the IT industry, each is unique in its own way.
A UI designer's job is the least technical, not requiring any programming experience and favoring creative and design experience and a visual design portfolio instead. It is also a job for a video game company and it is preferred that the applicant is knowledgeable about the gaming industry.
As a Head of Software Engineering is leading a team, it'll naturally be important that they have good leadership and management and are very involved with their respective team.
Software Development as a job requires the most technical expertise and experience, with the Developer needing to be proficient in many programming languages, frameworks, APIs and Cloud services.
The Embedded Software Engineer job is a from a company that works with racecars and motor sport, and is recommended for the applicant to have knowledge of how they work and be able to read circuit diagrams.
A Mechatronics Engineer is the most hardware-oriented out of all the jobs chosen, and it also unique in that it is in the field of robotics. Although some of the Engineer's responsibilities are related to software, the job advertisement does recommend having electrical engineering and workshop experience.

How similar or different are your career plans across the group?

With a few outliers, the majority of the jobs chosen by the team were software/development jobs that require technical proficiency and involve working in teams. That would seem to indicate that most members of the team have similar ideas of what makes an appealing job to them, and might also point to the team having similar skillsets and interests in IT. Having similar interests can unite a team and ease communication due to mutual understanding, but a lack of variety in expertise can restrict a team's ability to try different things if it doesn't fall within their skillset, as well as make it more likely for a team to get stuck into a certain way of doing things, which may be inefficient or outdated.
In short, Team Alpha has mostly similar career plans, but whether that will be beneficial or detrimental to the group remains to be seen.
