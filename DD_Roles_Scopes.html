Roles

Designating roles for each team member is essential in having a well-understood approach to any presented task. Appointing roles such as Lead developer and Technical Designer can give members a better understanding of what is expected from them and which areas they should exert themselves more in. In our team, the roles of each member were mostly consistent over all weeks. However, it is understood how these roles can easily change.
In Team Alpha, our Lead Developer/Technical Developer was Madhur. As he had the most experience and skill with HTML, Madhur handled most of the building of the website and the writing and designing of it.
Mark focused a lot on research and finding out how to turn some ideas for the website into reality, such as a working "Sign up" and "Log in" system. He also made a comprehensive project plan and took care of the more back-end aspects of the project.
Anna focused on writing and organising. Staying on top of everybody and ensuring work is understood and done on time, and typically taking the role of 'leader'. She was also in charge of the presentation and design of the project, specifically making mock-ups of the UI.
Jing also focused a lot on writing as well as pitching many good ideas for website design and function.
Muhammad filled in the blanks where needed, in writing as well as filling in the information on the website.
Although everyone had a role to focus on, certain members often felt like exploring beyond their field, such as Anna and Jing, who enjoyed giving their opinions on website design (which was fully understood by all team members). Other than their designated roles. All team members filled in the writing they were required to do. Scopes and Limits

Completing a project within a month will be difficult task, as most of group members are inexperienced in app development. We acknowledge that we will not be able to finish everything in our project, which is why we have set some limits for what we planned to deliver.
While this app would work best as a mobile app, we decided to use html and limit our project to a web application. Since we already had knowledge of html from our previous assignments, we wouldn't have to spend more time learning skills to develop a mobile app. If the app is successful on the web, we will work on creating a mobile version.
Early on, Madhur suggested using a Bootstrap stylesheet to save time on formatting our own CSS from scratch. This would also allow us to end up with a more professional looking result for the interface.
The prototype will focus on Planner, Shopping List, and Expense Manager features, and aim to give each basic functionality. Group chat was left out since it was low priority and would take too long to develop. We believe these features showcase our vision for the project, but it they take too long to develop we will prioritise the Planner, as it is the core feature.
Our team was initially planning to incorporate a backend, as Mark has some experience with databases. In the beginning, he took up the task of setting up a database and worked hard to find a solution. In meeting with our tutor, Luka, he said that it was better for people to see our idea's potential than to make everything work, and suggested us work more on the frontend. This also makes sense since none of us have experience with JavaScript, and it would be unrealistic to try and learn it in such a short time.
We will aim to have developed a prototype by the end of this period. It isn't supposed to be the final interface, have all the features, or demonstrate much functionality, but is more meant as a first look at the essence of our project and the potential it has with more time and development.

Rome was not build in a day. Our project illustrate that we has capability to complete a demanding project assuming we have enough time and abundant resources.
