Summary of interview with Dr. Tony Liu



Dr. Tony Liu

is a Data Scientist who specialises in Financial Analytics. He has worked as an advisor to Indonesian Government, state-owned enterprises, start-ups and conglomerates and has more than 25 years of professional experience in IT database technology and analytics. He was interviewed by Team Alpha, who asked him a few questions about the nature of his work.

As quality AI and machine learning relies on quality data, Tony's job is make sure that the data produced is up to standard and diagnose potential problems with the data. He also works with companies in advising them on what decisions to make and how to improve their performance. The majority of his work has to do with Data Piping, or making sure the data is in the right format, in the right place at the right time. The other part of it is in analytics, which is split into descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics. These types of modelling seek to answer the questions of what happened, why did it happen, what will happen, and how can you make it happen' in regards to data and business outcomes.

Before he became a Data Scientist, Tony had done a variety of different IT jobs. He had worked as a programmer in for factory machine before working his way up to being a Database Consultant and being put in charge of managing a team. He found that skillset required for team management was much different than the skillset for programming. It involved writing a lot of specifications for other people to work off of instead of doing it yourself, as well making sure your team delivered satisfactory results on time. After that he worked in a tax office for a while before receiving the opportunity to advise the Indonesian Ministry of Finance. The work for that job consisted of a lot of making speeches and writing reports. He also did work for the United Nations (UN) World Bank and International Monetary Fund (IMF), which was mostly about going to events and networking. All of these jobs ended up paving the way to where he is now, working as a data analytics advisor for bi-lateral trade between Australia and Indonesia.

Some financial background is good to have if you want to get into the Data Science field, according to Tony, since a lot of the time you will be working with companies and businesses and being able to understand the terminology and jargon will better help you to understand the company's needs and make more focused assessments with the data that you're working with. It also helps having that knowledge when figuring out with company to work for, as you can easily interpret the financial data that they release and find out whether this company is one worth working for.

Teamwork is something Tony finds very challenging to deal with. He compared the experience to that of an African hunter, where a team of people will go out to hunt but one person will end up doing the majority of the work (that is, tracking and chasing the animal until it dies of exhaustion) while the whole community profits. As he puts it, "If you are so unlucky that you are the African hunter, just be prepared. Teamwork is cruel." He also said that this kind of l setup is due to human nature and that if you end up in a such p osition you should at least ensure you get the appropriate amount of reward. But surprisingly, Tony's favourite part of his work is being able to brag about knowing things that other people don't.

Every day is different for Tony, but nowadays he mostly does data analysis, writes reports, builds models and works with a platform called KNIME, which is used for data mining, machine learning and data piping. He also likes to read and keep up to date with a financial news. When he worked as an advisor in Jakarta, he would often attend events with executives and condense data into a digestible form so that the business people can use it effectively. In actuality, being a data scientist is about recognizing company's needs, figuring out what information is most relevant to those needs and translating the data into knowledge and then into wisdom for companies to use.



Summary of interview with Mr. Ramandeep



Mr. Ramandeep

(referred to as Raman going forward) is an experienced software engineer with more than 15 years industry experience. Raman has worked in different IT companies managing very sophisticated and big projects. He is current working as Lead software architect designing, managing and developing the software architecture for super aquation funds investing company AMP. During his career he mainly focused on working on software developed for financial services via .NET framework which made him an expert in the field but his knowledge is not limited to this which far exceeds in other fields of IT.

Raman works for Australian Mutual Provident (AMP) which is a company that deals with banking , superannuation and investment funds. Specifically, he works as a Technical architect on their online platform North, which offers financial products and advice. He manages three teams that work to maintain the software as well as fulfill the needs from the product users and figure out what needs to be changed and the best way to implement those changes. He also does a lot of management, like breaking down and delegating tasks and making sure those tasks are done in a reasonable timeframe. While he used to do more coding earlier in his career, now he mostly reviews code and makes sure it's up to standard.

Being a Technical Architect allows Raman to work from home and take care of his family. He has been working on North for over 10 years and feels a sense of pride in his work and seeing how far the platform has come since then.

Maintaining a live service is never a smooth process ,and Raman frequently has to address production issues at a moment's notice. There are also many risks involved with making changes to your platform, as often something will break and potentially expose important data.

Before Raman started his career in IT, he was studying arts in high school. He did a degree in IT but was more interested in pursuing a career in journalism and mass communication. He tarted to reconsider going into IT after a few years as it was becoming a more lucrative field. He remembered how much he enjoyed creating websites in the past, and wanted to find a way to combine technology with journalism. Ultimately, he went back to Australia, decided he was going to pursue a career in IT full time, and broke into the industry by writing software for financial domains. He found the work really interesting and exciting, and it has gotten him to where he is today.

According to Raman, a lot of subjects studied in university will come in handy later down your career, no matter what industry you choose to go into. You can take the knowledge you learned years ago and apply in real situations. Even things that weren't related to your future job may give you inspiration or prove useful in other ways. Management skills are also important to have in a work environment, and should be developed while you are studying.

Most of the technology used in North has been relatively the same, with a few added features and changes in response user demand. As such, Raman sometimes has to look into new technologies, like cloud or third-party services, in order to figure out how to best integrate them into the existing platform.

Raman's experience with teamwork has been alright, and he has met some highly skilled people over his years of working in one. According to him, teamwork will involve bringing together many people with different skillsets, and it's important to recognize people's strengths and weaknesses and play to them. You will have to get used to people coming and going in teams, but if you try to be helpful to to others, it might help you in the long run.

Raman often has to deal with customers and clients who will often request features and have unreasonable expectations about how long said features might take to develop. A lot of the time he has to manage expectation and communicate with his clients while making a requested feature.

At it's core, Raman's work is about management and supervising a team, as well as meeting user's needs in the best way possible.
