Aims

Our aim for this project make a functioning prototype, MVP.
Broadly speaking, this project's aim is to give members of a shared household the tools to plan out how their house will be maintained. There are some other, more personal aims for this project. In developing RoomyPlanner, our team would hope to gain some experience both in collaboration as well as learning the tools of app development.
While we also have the goal of creating more transparency and making communication between users easier, we can't guarantee that our app will be able to solve underlying communication issues. Our app is only providing the tools, it's still up to the users to make this app work for their group.
We would need to develop multiple features in order to achieve our aim and give our users a complete experience. This next section will breakdown what needs to be done to implement each feature.
The Planner allows user to schedule and edit events on a calendar, assigning a name, date and person to each event. They can choose to schedule a chore, bill or other, which have their own property. Chores can be rotated between different people in a group, and bills can be split between users. All events can also be viewed in a list.
This feature is arguably the main feature of RoomyPlanner and would take priority in development because of its complexity and high demand from potential users. While there are a lot of moving parts to this feature, the main focus of development would be to make it able to schedule an event that shows up on the calendar. After the base functionality has been implemented, the different types of events and list view will be worked on next.
The shopping list is a note document that all members of the group can collaborate on. Users can make multiple lists for their needs, and all of them can be printed or transferred to other devices. They can also be customized to have different colours and styles.
This feature is often neglected by household management apps, but the base functionality is surprisingly simple to implement. After that comes the task of syncing the lists with other users devices, making them able to printed or downloaded, and making them customizable.
Expenses, like shopping or rent, can be recorded in a spreadsheet, which stores the name, date and amount of the expense. They will be automatically added up and compared against the user's current budget. Existing expenses are connected to the planner, and can be scheduled to be paid.
Since this feature is somewhat connected to the planner feature, it should be worked on either in conjunction with or after the planner feature. It is not of high priority and will likely be developed after the planner and shopping lists.
Group Chat allows users to message each other and share files. A group chat is automatically created for each group, but users can message each other individually as well.
This feature is the lowest priority and might not be developed if we don't have time. This kind of feature is common among many applications but is hard to build from scratch. Luckily, there are some templates available online for us to base our feature on.
Each feature should have enough time to develop and fill in more details but due to limited time we would focus on the important parts. The important parts of this project are bill management, shopping list, planner. If we have only enough time or resources for one of our goals, planner will be the import part.
Because paramount facts could link to planner and roomy can check on planner everyday avoid missing the critical information.
Messy shared spaces is tough. Roommate has total disregard for cleanliness, they may let their dirty cloth lay cross shared space or leave their dirty dishes in the sink for days. Plans and Progress

Introduction

This is a powerful tool to change how roommates organize their lifestyle. People usually feel lost when they move to a new place and start living with strangers. Each person has own their routine of waking up early or sleep late. Managing house business between roommates is important to maintain a healthy and conflict free environment.
Things like house cleaning, bills, grocery shopping are collaborative tasks which everyone either forgets or refuse to do. This is where "RoomyPlanner comes in to play. With RoomyPlanner you can assign chores around the house, create shopping lists, split and pay bills and other expenses, and set reminders for events.

The Story

Evern though this project idea emerged from the mind of one group member, but it became the group project because we all have gone through the similar life situations where living with roommates can be hard to get used to, especially for someone who has moved out for the first time. There can be people who are lazy and messy up the house while not doing any chores, or are annoying to others. The ones affected might not have the courage or be too busy to speak up and tell their roommates how they're bothering them, which only causes more frustration to build up. Even maintaining a chore roster can prove difficult, as people often forget if they're meant to do anything and there can often be communication breakdown since no one is usually in the same place at the same time.
This project has more a focus on general convenience, and seeks to make the experience of living with roommates more organized and stress free. Users can keep their chore schedules all in one place, and communicate with other roommates or manage things without needing face-to-face interaction. This can help to ease tensions between people who have different schedules or lifestyles living in the same house, and let everyone carry on with their lives without needing to worry about who's doing what or whether everyone knows what they need to do.

The Plan

We planned this project to be a self-sustained, fully functional smartphone app and a web-application with following features:

Planner

This is a shared calendar for all types of events that need to be completed, whether it be chores to be done, bills to be paid or other occasions. It can display the events for each week, month or year. The events calendar can also be displayed in a list view, which can be sorted either by date, person assigned, or type of event.
If someone wants to add an event, they can click the 'add' button on the bottom right or the day in the calendar they want to add the event to and choose from a pop-up whether it's a chore, bill or something else. This will send the user to another screen where they can type in a name and description, add participants by either selecting from a dropdown or typing a name, which will turn it into a search bar and deciding when it's due. When adding a bill, the user can choose from ones logged in the expense manager. When entering a chore, the user can choose to save it into a set that can more easily be selected the next time they want to assign one to someone.
Users can also make reoccurring events and choose how often they needs to be done. If they have assigned multiple people to the same task, they can also rotate who needs to do it each week and make custom rotation sets by dragging and dropping the assigned members in order. Users can also attach files to certain events; for example if they have to do with meal prep, someone can attach a recipe or shopping list.
Events can be edited by hovering over the chore and clicking 'edit' from a pop up that appears. This will send the user to the 'add' screen to make and apply changes.
Notifications will remind users who have unfinished tasks to do them, or give them the option to do them later. Once they have completed a task, users can mark it complete either from the home screen or the calendar.

Shopping List

This is essentially a collaborative notepad where users can make and add items to a main shopping list, or make separate ones for individuals or special occasions. They can also save shopping lists for certain recipes as well as the instructions to make them.
Users can type in the name of the item, how much of it they need (or choose from a drop down menu) and any other details about the item. Each member's contributions are labelled so all the house members can see who made what suggestion.
When an item on the shopping list, is acquired, it can be crossed off the shopping list and the initials of the person who got the item will be shown. The shopping lists can be marked as once-off or reoccurring. If a shopping list is entirely crossed off it will be moved to a separate tab of past shopping lists unless it is marked as reoccurring. Users can also customize their shopping list by changing the colour, font or style to differentiate one shopping list from the others. These shopping lists can be printed, downloaded or transferred to other devices for easier access while on the go.

Expense Management

The user can get in touch the renter to set up the dates for rent each month, which will be displayed on the calendar. Users will be able to schedule expenses on the planner where they can divide them with their roommates, and a progress bar will display how much of the expense has been paid.
Notifications can be turned on to remind the user of approaching payment dates. It can be synced with the user's bank account to allow the user to pay quickly from the app. Expenses can also be logged with separate tabs and calculated against the users current savings if they have the app synced with their bank account.

Group Chat

A group chat feature that functions similarly to other messaging apps, allowing roommates to interact, inform others of changes to the roster, or tell each other if they have commitments that prevent them from doing chores or paying bills and allocate them to others if necessary.
This can be accessed from the sidebar, notifications drop-down, or push notifications. A group chat is automatically formed when a group starts, but individual members can be contacted by clicking on their name and starting a chat with them.

General features

The user mainly uses a sidebar to navigate between features, while notifications and profile are accessed from icons on a nav menu on the top of the screen.
When the user clicks on notifications a dropdown appears displaying recent notifications, and any unread ones will be marked as read. The user can click on a notification in the dropdown to go to the relevant page.
When the user clicks on profile a dropdown appears with options for going their profile page, settings and logging out. In the profile page users can customize aspects of their profile, like their username and profile picture. In settings they can set various things like notifications and privacy to their preference.

Login/Sign up System

It is fair to say the "tech team" procrastinated somewhat before we came up with a login process that we could confidently embed in the system. Our original plan was to hook in a database (on the cloud) that would keep track of registered users. We decided, after talking with our Tutor, that this design may not be realistic for us to implement given the timeframe. We designed a system using a Jscript that did implement a read only registered user file that perhaps looks like a database for prototyping purposes. The validation script is validLogon.js, the pseudo database is people.js

The Login process checks that the email address and password has a match in people.js:
The sign On process checks:

  • First and last Name is more than 2 characters or more.
  • Email address is a valid format ie xxxxx@xxxx.xxx.
  • Password must be 8-16 characters containing 1 upper case, 1 lower case a and a numeric
